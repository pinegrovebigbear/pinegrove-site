Local Guide

A guide to Big Bear City, from the people who live it

Not a tourism-board rundown of the whole valley — this is specifically about Big Bear City, the quieter east end where Pine Grove Cabin sits, and how it differs from Big Bear Lake Village.

Big Bear City vs. Big Bear Lake Village

These two get lumped together constantly, but they're distinct places with different feels. Big Bear Lake Village, on the south shore, is the postcard strip — restaurants, shops, the pier, and most of the tourist traffic. Big Bear City sits on the north/east end of the valley, in unincorporated San Bernardino County. It's residential, quieter, and closer to the airport, Snow Summit's back-side access, and the lake's less-crowded north shore. If you want village nightlife outside your door, stay in the Village. If you want a quiet street to park three cars on and a five-minute drive to the trailhead, that's Big Bear City.

Getting outside

Snow Summit & Bear Mountain

Both ski resorts are a short drive from Big Bear City — Snow Summit's back routes are noticeably closer from this side of the valley than from the Village.

North Shore trails

Quieter hiking and biking access than the more crowded south-shore trailheads near the Village.

Big Bear Lake

Multiple public access points reachable without fighting Village parking.

Elevation & weather

Big Bear sits above 6,700 ft. Expect cooler nights than the Southern California valleys year-round, and snow-ready roads in winter.

Planning tip

Short-term rentals in Big Bear City are permitted differently than the Village

Big Bear City falls under San Bernardino County's unincorporated STR permitting system, distinct from the City of Big Bear Lake's rules. Pine Grove Cabin operates under active San Bernardino County STR Permit CESTRP-2021-00256, with a maximum overnight occupancy of 7 guests and 3 vehicles.

When to visit

Winter (Dec–Mar)

Skiing and snow play at Snow Summit and Bear Mountain. Roads can require chains — check conditions before driving up.

Summer (Jun–Aug)

Cooler than the valley below, with hiking, mountain biking, and lake access. The cabin has no A/C, but summer evenings at 6,700 ft rarely need it.

Fall & Spring

The shoulder seasons are quieter and cheaper, with fall color and fewer crowds on the trails.
